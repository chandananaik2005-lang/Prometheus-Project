The Rise of Agentic Systems: Beyond Large Language Models

EV
Dr. Elena Vance
Oct 24, 2024 · 8 min read
🔖
↗
🧠

The landscape of artificial intelligence is undergoing a seismic shift. While the last two years were dominated by Large Language Models (LLMs) like GPT-4, the industry is now pivoting towards agentic systems — software that can plan, reason, and execute complex workflows autonomously.

Unlike traditional chatbots, an agentic system possesses a sense of agency. It can break down a high-level goal — such as "organise a multi-city research trip" — into constituent tasks: booking flights, researching local experts, synthesising interview notes, and managing a budget.

The core architectural difference lies in the feedback loops. Agentic systems use recursive reasoning to check their own work, browse the live web, and interact with software APIs. They are effectively becoming the 'operating system' for digital labour.

However, this autonomy brings significant challenges in safety and alignment. If an agent is empowered to move funds or modify code independently, the margin for error shrinks to near zero. We are now entering an era where the 'human-in-the-loop' is transitioning to a 'human-on-the-loop' supervisor.

"The question is no longer what the machine can say, but what the machine can do."
